About Apple

Apple is a multinational company that makes not only smartphones, but also laptops, desktops, audio equipment, software and even R&D on semiconductors, consumer electronics and microprocessors as well. Apple has been at the forefront of technology with its iPhone and Mac. As of 2018, there are about 500 Apple retail stores all over the globe. In 2017, the total revenue generated by Apple was almost $230 billion. The total value of net assets that Apple possesses is around $375 billion. As of 2017, there are about 123,000 Apple employees currently working in the company. In April 2018, Apple unveiled its new headquarters, known as the Apple Park, in California USA.

Apple History

Apple was founded in the year 1976, by co-founders Steven Paul Jobs and Steve Wozniak. In 1977, the company was incorporated as Apple Computers Inc. Apple slowly started hiring talent and started producing various types of computers. The Apple II was a blockbuster hit among technology enthusiasts at that time. However, in the 1980s, Steve Jobs left Apple and helped Pixar to grow - which is now a part of Disney. Steve Jobs returned to Apple at the end of the 1990s and tried to make amends for the damages done by the previous Apple CEOs. Apple then went onto releasing groundbreaking products like the iPod, iPhone and even the iPad. As of 2018, Apple is the most valuable Fortune 500 companies in the world.
